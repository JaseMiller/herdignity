A Sustainable Model for Reducing Maternal Mortality: Evidence from the SHARE Project in Sub-Himalayan India

Dr. George is a physician and bioethicist from India who specializes in community health.Currently she is the Executive Director of The Centre for Bioethics in India, which was founded by 15 institutions to be a Christian voice upholding the sanctity of life and the dignity of humans.

Dr. Jameela George will discuss the medical causes of maternal mortality as well as the socio-cultural, economic, and topographical factors that contribute to maternal mortality in rural India. She will also introduce the SHARE project, which features a dyanmic, results-oriented, replicable emergency referral system that has positively impacted maternal mortality rates in villages where it has been implemented. It is our hope that Dr. George's sustainable, community-based model might be adopted in other contexts to reduce maternal mortality and promote the health and dignity of women.
